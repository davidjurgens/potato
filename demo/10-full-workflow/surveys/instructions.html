Fact-Checking Annotation Guidelines

In this task, you will evaluate the veracity of common claims. For each claim, determine whether it is:

  • True — The claim is factually accurate based on current scientific consensus.
  • False — The claim is factually inaccurate or a known misconception.
  • Unverifiable — The claim cannot be definitively confirmed or denied with available evidence.

Guidelines

  1. Read each claim carefully before making a judgment.
  2. Consider the most widely accepted scientific evidence.
  3. Use the confidence slider to indicate how sure you are of your answer.
  4. If a claim is partially true, consider whether the core assertion is accurate.

Examples

Claim Label Reasoning
"Water boils at 100 degrees Celsius at sea level." True This is a well-established physical fact.
"Bats are blind." False All bat species have eyes and can see. Some use echolocation in addition to vision.
